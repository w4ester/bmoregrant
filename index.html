🎯 Core Mission: From Consumer to Producer

Transform Baltimore families from AI consumers to AI producers by teaching children (ages 12-17), young adults (18-25), and parents (26-55) to create working AI tools together, establishing a producer mindset before consumer habits solidify.

💡 Why This Matters

Creating a new generation of AI producers (not users) who own their tools, their data, and have choices for technology to be tools for them to build economic value for families and communities. Starting at age 12, teaching families together, using local models that work forever - this is how we shift to a producer-first, consumer-second mindset, before it becomes even more challenging.

🤝 Partnership Structure

This is a formal partnership between Baltimore City Nonprofit (workforce development & community coordination) and Baltimore City (community partners)

🛠️ What They'll Create

  • Families Together: Individual and community solutions with AI (childcare on-site)

Baltimore AI Producers Lab - Grant Strategy

Executive Summary

Project: Deploy family-based AI production training to 100 Baltimore families (250+ people, ages 12-55) through community workshops powered by locally-hosted AI models.

Partnership: Formal collaboration between Baltimore City Nonprofit (workforce development & community coordination) and Baltimore City (community partners).

Innovation: Unlike cloud-based programs, we teach families to BUILD AI tools from pre-trained models, not just use them. Starting at age 12, before consumer habits form. Local models = privacy, zero cost, offline capability, true ownership.

Request: $250,000 for 6-month pilot demonstration

The Producer Gap

❌ Current State

  • 70% of Baltimore teens use ChatGPT for homework, zero know how it works
  • Families pay $20/month to be AI consumers
  • Only 3% of low-income youth have built tech tools
  • By age 18, consumer habits already formed

✓ Our Approach

  • Start Early: Age 12 is when producer identity forms
  • Family Unit: Household culture change, not individual training
  • Real Creation: Build 2-3 working tools with economic value
  • True Ownership: Local models mean tools work forever, offline, with no subscriptions

7-Module Producer Curriculum

Module 1: AI Demystification

Understand AI is math, not magic. Build tiny model. Project: Teach others how AI works.

Module 2: Prompt Engineering

Master system prompts and A/B testing. Project: Custom homework helper.

Module 3: Tool Builder

No-code interface for AI tools. Project: Resume improver or essay reviewer.

Module 4: Document Analysis

Upload transcripts, extract info. Project: Skills extractor, benefits checker.

Module 5: Real-World APIs

Connect to job boards, transit. Project: Auto-apply tool.

Module 6: Fine-Tuning

Customize models on your data. Project: Family-specific AI.

Module 7: Deploy & Monetize

Share tools, earn income. Project: Producer portfolio.

Why This is More Powerful

1. Start Young = Form Producer Identity Early

The Problem with Adult Training:

  • By age 18, people have 10+ years of being passive tech consumers
  • Consumer habits are deeply ingrained
  • Harder to shift mindset from "I use ChatGPT" to "I build AI tools"

The Family Advantage:

  • Kids ages 8-12 haven't formed consumer habits yet
  • Teaching them to BUILD before they learn to USE changes everything
  • They grow up seeing AI as something they control, not magic

2. Multi-Generational = Sustainable Change

Why Families Work Better:

  • Parents and children learn together → mutual reinforcement
  • Shared projects create lasting household culture change
  • Kids teach parents, parents support kids = continuous learning

3. Producer vs. Consumer = Economic Power

AI Consumers AI Producers
Pay $20/month for ChatGPT Build their own tools for free
Use AI for basic tasks Create AI solutions for community
Limited by what vendors provide Customize everything for their needs
No income from AI skills Can freelance, automate, create value

Why Local Models Matter for Families

1. Child Safety & Privacy

The Problem with Cloud AI: Kids share names, schools, personal struggles. Data goes to OpenAI, Google, Microsoft. Parents have zero control.

Our Solution: Everything runs locally. No data sent to cloud ever. Parents see exactly what happens to their child's data.

2. Economic Sustainability

The Subscription Trap: ChatGPT costs $20/month per person = $60/month for family of 3. Over 10 years: $7,200 just for access.

Our Model: One-time hardware purchase. Families build tools they own forever. No ongoing costs, ever.

3. Offline Capability

Baltimore Digital Divide: 40% of families lack reliable broadband.

Our Solution: Workshops run on local network. Take-home laptops work offline.

4. Educational Transparency

Cloud AI = Black Box: Kids see AI as magic.

Local Models = Learning Tool: Kids see model parameters, training data, experiment with fine-tuning. Understand AI is engineering, not magic.
