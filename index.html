🎯 The Big Idea: From Consumer to Producer

Most AI training teaches people to use AI. We teach families to create with AI. By starting with children (ages 8+) and including parents, we shift entire households from passive consumers to active producers—building tools, solving real problems, and generating economic value.

💡 Why This Matters

AI adoption is creating a producer-consumer divide. Fortune 500 employees use AI to automate tasks and create value. But families in Baltimore are stuck using ChatGPT for homework help. We're training the next generation to build the tools, not just use them—and starting young, before consumer habits solidify.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Who We Serve

  • 150 Baltimore families (450+ individuals total)
  • Youth (ages 8-17): Future AI producers, learning to build before they learn to consume
  • Young Adults (18-25): Career-starters who need AI creation skills
  • Parents (26-45): Supporting children while building their own skills
  • Focus: Families without college degrees, median income <$45K

🛠️ What They'll Create

  • Youth: AI games, homework helpers, creative tools, math tutors
  • Young Adults: Resume builders, job automators, portfolio generators
  • Parents: Budget tools, benefits navigators, side-hustle assistants
  • Family: Community problem-solving tools
  • Outcome: Every participant builds 2-3 working AI tools

7-Module Producer Curriculum

Module 1: AI Demystification

Understand AI is math, not magic. Build tiny model. Project: Teach others how AI works.

Module 2: Prompt Engineering

Master system prompts and A/B testing. Project: Custom homework helper.

Module 3: Tool Builder

No-code interface for AI tools. Project: Resume improver or essay reviewer.

Module 4: Document Analysis

Upload transcripts, extract info. Project: Skills extractor, benefits checker.

Module 5: Real-World APIs

Connect to job boards, transit. Project: Auto-apply tool.

Module 6: Fine-Tuning

Customize models on your data. Project: Family-specific AI.

Module 7: Deploy & Monetize

Share tools, earn income. Project: Producer portfolio.

  • Teaching them to BUILD before they learn to USE changes everything
  • They grow up seeing AI as something they control, not magic

2. Multi-Generational = Sustainable Change

Why Families Work Better:

  • Parents and children learn together → mutual reinforcement
  • Shared projects create lasting household culture change
  • Kids teach parents, parents support kids = continuous learning

Why Local Models Matter for Families

1. Child Safety & Privacy

The Problem with Cloud AI: Kids share names, schools, personal struggles. Data goes to OpenAI, Google, Microsoft. Parents have zero control.

Our Solution: Everything runs locally. No data sent to cloud ever. Parents see exactly what happens to their child's data.

2. Economic Sustainability

The Subscription Trap: ChatGPT costs $20/month per person = $60/month for family of 3. Over 10 years: $7,200 just for access.

Our Model: One-time hardware purchase. Families build tools they own forever. No ongoing costs, ever.

3. Offline Capability

Baltimore Digital Divide: 40% of families lack reliable broadband.

Our Solution: Workshops run on local network. Take-home laptops work offline.

4. Educational Transparency

Cloud AI = Black Box: Kids see AI as magic.

Local Models = Learning Tool: Kids see model parameters, training data, experiment with fine-tuning. Understand AI is engineering, not magic.
