Baltimore AI Producers Lab - Grant Strategy

Executive Summary

Project: Deploy family-based AI production training to 30 Baltimore families (up to 100 residents, ages 14-55) through community workshops powered by locally-hosted AI models.

Partnership: Formal collaboration between Baltimore City Nonprofit (workforce development & community coordination) and Baltimore City (community partners).

Innovation: Unlike cloud-based programs, we teach families to BUILD AI tools from pre-trained models, not just use them. Starting at age 14, before consumer habits form. Local models = privacy, zero cost, offline capability, true ownership.

Request: $250,000 for 6-month pilot demonstration

The Producer Gap: Real Baltimore Data

‚ùå Current State (2024 Baltimore City Data)

  • 20,000 Baltimore families with children under 17 lack both broadband AND computers at home
  • 21.7% of Black youth ages 16-24 are "Opportunity Youth" (not in school or work) vs. 7.7% White youth
  • College enrollment declined from 54.6% (2019) to 44.9% (2022)
  • 58.3% of Baltimore graduates need college remediation vs. 32.7% statewide
  • Zero tech production skills: Youth consume AI tools but don't learn to build them

Sources: Baltimore City Youth Data Scorecard 2024, Abell Foundation Digital Divide Report

‚úì Our Approach

  • Start Early: Age 14, before dropping out or becoming Opportunity Youth
  • Family Unit: 30 families (up to 100 residents) learn together - household culture change
  • Real Creation: Build 2-3 working AI tools with economic value
  • True Ownership: Local models work offline (addresses 40.7% without broadband)
  • Economic Pathway: Alternative to college remediation - producer skills from day one

7-Module Producer Curriculum

Module 1: AI Demystification

Understand AI is math, not magic. Build tiny model. Project: Teach others how AI works.

Module 2: Prompt Engineering

Master system prompts and A/B testing. Project: Custom homework helper.

Module 3: Tool Builder

No-code interface for AI tools. Project: Resume improver or essay reviewer.

Module 4: Document Analysis

Upload transcripts, extract info. Project: Skills extractor, benefits checker.

Module 5: Real-World APIs

Connect to job boards, transit. Project: Auto-apply tool.

Module 6: Fine-Tuning

Customize models on your data. Project: Family-specific AI.

Module 7: Deploy & Monetize

Share tools, earn income. Project: Producer portfolio.

Why Local Models Matter for Families

1. Child Safety & Privacy

The Problem with Cloud AI: Kids share names, schools, personal struggles. Data goes to OpenAI, Google, Microsoft. Parents have zero control.

Our Solution: Everything runs locally. No data sent to cloud ever. Parents see exactly what happens to their child's data.

2. Economic Sustainability

The Subscription Trap: ChatGPT costs $20/month per person = $60/month for family of 3. Over 10 years: $7,200 just for access. Many of 20,000 Baltimore families without computers can't afford this.

Our Model: One-time hardware investment. Families build tools they own forever. Median earnings without diploma ($29,967) means subscriptions are unsustainable.

3. Offline Capability (Baltimore Digital Divide Data)

Baltimore Reality: 96,000 households (40.7%) lack wireline broadband service. Baltimore ranks 29th out of 33 cities for broadband adoption.

Our Solution: Workshops run on local network. Take-home mini PCs work offline. 20,000 families with kids lacking tech can participate.

4. Educational Transparency

Cloud AI = Black Box: Kids see AI as magic.

Local Models = Learning Tool: Kids see model parameters, training data, experiment with fine-tuning. Understand AI is engineering, not magic.
